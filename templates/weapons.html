Introduction to Indian Ancient Weapons

Indian ancient weapons are a testament to the rich history and martial traditions of the subcontinent. From the mighty Khanda to the versatile Chakram, these weapons were not only tools of war but also symbols of power, honor, and craftsmanship.

Khanda

The Khanda is a double-edged straight sword originating from the Indian subcontinent. It was widely used by Rajput warriors.

Talwar

The Talwar is a curved sword from India, known for its sharp blade and use by Mughal and Rajput warriors.

Chakram

The Chakram is a circular throwing weapon from India, famously used by the Sikhs and Rajputs.

Gada

The Gada is a heavy mace used in ancient India, often associated with Lord Hanuman and wrestlers.

Trishula

The Trishula is a trident spear associated with Lord Shiva. It symbolizes the balance of creation, preservation, and destruction.

Bagh Nakh

The Bagh Nakh, or "tiger claws," is a claw-like weapon used for slashing and tearing. It was famously used by Shivaji Maharaj.
